About Me

My name is Jackson A. Henry and I graduated from Baylor University with a Bachelor's of Science in Applied Mathematics.

Currently, I work as a Warehouse Associate at Amazon and as a mathematics instructor at Mathnasium.

In the future, I hope to become a full-stack developer. I am currently in the Coding Bootcamp at UT Austin to further my skills in software development.

Furthermore, I am planning to pursue a master's degree in Computer Science, with research emphasis in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.



Work Experience

Currently, I am working at Amazon as a Warehouse Associate in a sortation center.

My main job at Amazon is to pick routes from a shelf and transport them to a staging area as quickly and safely as possible.

In addition to route picking, I help drivers load their packages, assist with lane operations, help with sortation, and occasionally lead cardboard disposal operations.

At this job, I strive to help as many of my fellow associates and managers as possible, while getting my tasks done as efficiently as possible.







In addition to Amazon, I currently am a Math tutor at a Mathnasium learning center.

At Mathnasium, I work with students in order to help them improve their skills in math. Every student who comes into the center takes an assessment, which helps us pinpoint where they are at in math and where their knowledge "gaps" are. We create a learning plan which helps the students fill in those gaps. In addition, we will devise new methods which will help students better understand what they are learning and how they can apply it.

One of the things we do at Mathnasium, is to update parents on their students progress. Since parents are paying a lot of money to send their kids to Mathnasium, we believe that it is important for them to see how we are helping their kids.

Furthermore, we offer private tutoring for older students. Last year, I worked with a student in Pre-Calculus and Statistics. Occasionally, I will help him out with Calculus.




During college, I was a part of the Golden Wave Band for four years. My senior year, I was selected to be one of four equipment managers.

As an Equipment Manager, I was responsible for checking out all school-owned instruments. Myself, and three other equipment managers used Microsoft Excel in order to maintain all forms and over $1 million worth of equipment.

Working with the band staff, directors, and other equipment managers, we created a more streamlined process for transporting equipment. As a result of our efforts, we had no lost equipment at any BUGWB event.







Organizations involved with

Band of the Hills is an all-ages, all-skill level community band. Started in 1998, the band has performed throughout Central Texas.

I joined the band in high school. After graduating from Baylor, I rejoined the band. Getting to play great music and meeting great people is one of the biggest reasons why I continue to perform with this organization.


The Knights of Columbus is a Catholic fraternal organization. The organization was founded in 1882 by Father Michael J McGivney in New Haven, Connecticut. Today, there are almost 2 million men who are Knights. In addition, there are almost 15,000 college councils, including over 300 on college campuses.

I joined this organization during my freshman year at Baylor. As a sophmore, I became the advocate for the council and remained in that position until graduation. Meanwhile, I became a 4th degree knight halfway through my junior year. After graduating from Baylor, I transferred to the council in my home parish.




Boy Scouting has played a big role in my life. I joined Scouting as a Wolf in 2nd grade, and stuck with it until aging out in 2012.

While in Boy Scouting, I earned the rank of Eagle Scout. After earning the rank of Eagle Scout, I stayed active with the troop and continued to earn merit badges and Eagle Palms.

Currently, I am an adult leader with Troop 345 in Round Rock. I give back to scouting by participating on Board of Reivews for scouts who are advancing to their next rank.

Duolingo is my favorite site to get a basic grasp of a new foreign language. I started using this site in order to improve my Spanish speaking skills. Today I use the site to practice other languages including French, German, and Chinese.

One of the motivating factors of Duolingo is the gamification. Duolingo records how many consecutive days you have spent using the site. In addition, Duolingo has in-lesson grading which will allow you to see what you are doing well and what you need to practice on more. Furthermore, Duolingo keeps track of how many xp you have on a language, what level you are at on the language, and your total xp.
